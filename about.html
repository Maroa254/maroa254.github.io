ABOUT US

Eastern Africa Students and Youth Organization (EASYO) is a regional students and youth organization which was formed by initiation of Students Union of Arsi University and students’ union of other Ethiopian Universities in Consultation with national students and youth association/union in the region. It was aimed to create cooperation and integration among the young generation of the region, Africa and worldwide. The call for formation of this organization was sent to national students’ association of East African countries and immediately got positive responses from Burundi National Students Union, Uganda National Students Association, Rwanda National Students Association, Sudanese National Students Association, Tanzanian Higher Learning Students Organization, Somalian Higher Education Students Union and University of Nairobi Students Association. Social media played a crucial role in the formation of this organization because until registration of this organization we didn’t make face to face meeting. Our first face to face meeting for EASYO members was taken place in Rabat, Morocco on December 20, 2020 where a discussion, consultation and a way forward was discussed in detail with the founding members, invited guests and new members of this organization. On this meeting students and young leaders from Ethiopia, Uganda, Somalia, Kenya, Rwanda, Djibouti, Sudan, Tanzania, Mauritius, South Sudan, Seychelles, and Burundi attended. The meeting was leaded by the EASYO acting president, Mr Eyasu Getu from Ethiopia and EASYO Acting Vice chair Mr Abdimalik nor Omar from Somalia and was mediated by Representatives from Uganda and Tanzania. There were also a guests from South Africa, Denmark and other countries who represent Civil society organizations which work on students and youth. The focuses are Youth and Students unity to find solutions to our challenges since we realized, we have common bottle necks. We formed this organization to tackle our common problems together because youth are very vulnerable to many developmental challenges.

EASYO

shall focus on 10 strategical core activities:

1) Youth empowerment.

2) Students right.

3) Youth rehabilitation.

4) HIV/AIDS.

5) Democracy and good governance.

6) Environmental and climate changes.

7) Innovation.

8) Gender advocacy.

9) Migration and mobility.

10) Pan-Africanism.

The aims and objectives for which organization is formed are to:

• Unite and facilitate reconciliation between and among the students and youths of Eastern Africa

• Achieve sustainable peace, development, culture of dialogue, tolerance, compromise and good governance

• Fight ignorance, disease, and all forms of discrimination at the country level and regional.

• Enhance integrity, transparency, and accountability in the management of public affairs which provides a stable leadership skill to youth.

• Promote sub-regional and regional integration efforts through exchange programs in the field of politics, agriculture, health, education and business.

• Promote interchange, contact and cooperation among the universities and other institutions of higher education in Africa.

• Encourage and empower members of the organization to address developmental challenges and employment constrains to become effective and responsible members of their respective countries.

• Promote linkage with international higher education community

• Support cultural and traditional institutions

• Establish a policy of zero tolerance on corruption among youths

• Support and promote the struggle for the right of women, children, disabled students and all other marginalized groups through implementation of affirmative action

• Cooperate with other organization of students and youth in Africa or World Wide with similar objective where possible work together to achieve the social objectives.

• To promote gender and social equity in policies and delivery of services on higher education.

• To strengthen and support students’ associations at national level and students’ associations in Universities and high schools of Eastern African countries

• To help and support elders and marginalized groups by youth and students’ mobilizations
